CROSS-FUNCTIONAL TEAM VS STAKEHOLDERS

1. Cross-functional Team:
Cross-functional teams are groups of people from different roles such as developers, QA, 
designers, DevOps, and product managers who directly work together to build and deliver a product or feature. 
They are responsible for execution, development, testing, and release.


Example:
- Developers
- QA Engineers
- UI/UX Designers
- DevOps Engineers
- Product Managers
- TPM / Project Manager

Key Point:
They are directly involved in daily execution and delivery of work.

------------------------------------------------------------

2. Stakeholders:
Stakeholders are individuals or groups who have an interest in the project or are impacted by it. 
They do not work on daily execution but influence decisions, provide requirements, and give feedback.

Example:
- Business Leaders / Executives
- Customers / End Users
- Sales & Marketing Teams
- Finance Team
- Legal / Compliance Team
- Support / Customer Success

Key Point:
They influence the project but are not part of daily execution.

------------------------------------------------------------

3. Key Difference:

Cross-functional Team → Builds and delivers the product (Execution)
Stakeholders → Guide, approve, and influence the product (Decision & Feedback)

------------------------------------------------------------

4. TPM Perspective:
As a Technical Project Manager, I coordinate with cross-functional teams 
for execution and align with stakeholders for requirements, priorities, 
and approvals to ensure smooth, predictable, and high-quality delivery.
