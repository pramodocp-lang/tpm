PMP INTERVIEW QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
Part 1 (Questions 1–20)

---

1. What is PMP?

---

Answer:
PMP (Project Management Professional) is a globally recognized certification offered by the Project Management Institute (PMI). It validates a professional's ability to lead and manage projects using predictive, Agile, and hybrid approaches.

---

2. What is a project?

---

Answer:
A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Every project has a defined start, end, scope, budget, and objectives.

---

3. What is project management?

---

Answer:
Project management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to meet project objectives while balancing scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, and risks.

---

4. What is the role of a Project Manager?

---

Answer:
A Project Manager is responsible for:

* Planning the project
* Managing stakeholders
* Leading the team
* Tracking progress
* Managing risks and issues
* Ensuring successful project delivery

---

5. What are the five Process Groups?

---

Answer:

1. Initiating
2. Planning
3. Executing
4. Monitoring & Controlling
5. Closing

---

6. What are the ten Knowledge Areas?

---

Answer:

1. Integration
2. Scope
3. Schedule
4. Cost
5. Quality
6. Resource
7. Communication
8. Risk
9. Procurement
10. Stakeholder Management

---

7. What is a Project Charter?

---

Answer:
The Project Charter formally authorizes the project. It defines the project's purpose, objectives, scope, sponsor, budget, high-level risks, and gives authority to the Project Manager.

---

8. Who creates the Project Charter?

---

Answer:
The Project Sponsor usually creates or approves the Project Charter before the project officially begins.

---

9. What is a Stakeholder?

---

Answer:
A stakeholder is any individual, group, or organization that can influence or be affected by the project.

---

10. What is Scope?

---

Answer:
Scope defines all the work required to deliver the project's objectives and deliverables. Anything outside the approved scope is considered out of scope.

---

11. What is Scope Creep?

---

Answer:
Scope creep is the uncontrolled addition of new requirements without proper approval or change management, which can affect schedule, cost, and quality.

---

12. What is a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)?

---

Answer:
A WBS is a hierarchical decomposition of the project into smaller, manageable work packages.

---

13. What is a Milestone?

---

Answer:
A milestone is a significant point or event in a project used to measure progress. It has zero duration.

---

14. What is a Deliverable?

---

Answer:
A deliverable is a measurable output or result produced during the project.

---

15. What is the Critical Path?

---

Answer:
The Critical Path is the longest sequence of dependent activities that determines the minimum project duration. Delays on the critical path delay the entire project.

---

16. What is Float (Slack)?

---

Answer:
Float is the amount of time an activity can be delayed without affecting the project completion date.

Formula:
Float = Late Start – Early Start

---

17. What is Risk?

---

Answer:
A risk is an uncertain event or condition that may positively or negatively affect project objectives.

---

18. What is an Issue?

---

Answer:
An issue is a problem that has already occurred and requires immediate action, whereas a risk is only a possibility.

---

19. What is RAID?

---

Answer:
RAID stands for:
R – Risks
A – Assumptions
I – Issues
D – Dependencies

It is a project management tool used to monitor and track key project information.

---

20. What is Change Management?

---

Answer:
Change Management is the structured process of evaluating, approving, implementing, 
and communicating changes to project scope, schedule, cost, or requirements while minimizing project impact.
