=========================================================
TPM AFTER PLANNING PHASE (EXECUTION STARTS)
=========================================================

1. WHAT IS AFTER PLANNING PHASE?

After planning, the project moves into execution where
actual development, testing, and delivery work begins.

Goal:
- Execute planned work
- Track progress
- Manage risks and issues
- Ensure on-time delivery

=========================================================

2. TPM ROLE AFTER PLANNING

A TPM shifts from "planning owner" to "execution driver".

Key responsibilities:

1. Start sprint execution
2. Track daily progress
3. Manage dependencies
4. Resolve blockers
5. Monitor risks and issues
6. Communicate with stakeholders
7. Ensure delivery alignment

=========================================================

3. SPRINT EXECUTION START

TPM ensures:

- Sprint goals are clear
- Tasks are assigned
- Teams are aligned
- Capacity is confirmed

Example:
Sprint 1 Goal = Login Module + User Registration

=========================================================

4. DAILY TRACKING (VERY IMPORTANT)

TPM monitors daily:

- What is completed?
- What is in progress?
- What is blocked?

Tools:
- Jira board
- Daily stand-ups
- Team updates

=========================================================

5. BLOCKER MANAGEMENT

Common blockers:

- API not ready
- Environment issues
- Dependency delays
- Resource unavailability

TPM ACTION:

- Identify blocker early
- Coordinate with teams
- Escalate if needed
- Remove dependency roadblocks

=========================================================

6. RISK & ISSUE MANAGEMENT

TPM continuously tracks:

RISKS (future problems):
- Resource may go on leave
- Vendor delay expected

ISSUES (current problems):
- Server down
- Feature broken in testing

=========================================================

7. DEPENDENCY MANAGEMENT

Example:

Task: QA Testing
Depends on: Development completion

TPM ensures:
- Dependencies are tracked in Jira
- No idle time between teams
- Work flows smoothly

=========================================================

8. STAKEHOLDER COMMUNICATION

TPM updates:

A. Product Owner
- Sprint progress
- Feature completion

B. Business Leaders
- Project health (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Risks & delays

C. Teams
- Daily coordination
- Priority alignment

=========================================================

9. STATUS REPORTING

Weekly Report Includes:

- Completed tasks
- In-progress tasks
- Blocked tasks
- Risks
- Issues
- Next steps

Example:

Project Status: GREEN
Progress: 60%
Risks: 2
Issues: 1

=========================================================

10. METRICS TRACKED

- Sprint velocity
- Completion %
- Defect count
- Blocker count
- Cycle time

=========================================================

11. TPM INTERVIEW ANSWER

Q: What do you do after planning phase?

Answer:

After the planning phase, I focus on execution and tracking.
I ensure sprint execution starts smoothly, monitor daily
progress through Jira, manage risks and dependencies, and
resolve blockers. I coordinate between development, QA,
and product teams while providing regular updates to
stakeholders. My goal is to ensure timely and predictable
delivery.

=========================================================

12. ONE-LINE SUMMARY

After planning, TPM ensures execution, tracking, risk
management, dependency handling, and continuous
stakeholder communication.
