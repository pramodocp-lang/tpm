=========================================================
TPM CORE ADVANCED CONCEPTS (INTERVIEW CRITICAL)
=========================================================

1. DEPENDENCY MANAGEMENT

What is it?
Dependency management is tracking relationships between
tasks, teams, and systems where one work item depends on
another.

Example:
- QA testing depends on development completion
- UI depends on design approval
- Backend depends on API integration

TPM ROLE:
- Identify dependencies early
- Track them in Jira/Excel
- Ensure no idle time between teams
- Escalate delays immediately

=========================================================

2. RISK MANAGEMENT

What is it?
Risk is a potential future problem that may impact the project.

Types of risks:
- Resource risk (people unavailability)
- Technical risk (system complexity)
- Schedule risk (delay possibility)
- External risk (vendor/API delay)

TPM ROLE:
- Maintain risk register
- Define mitigation plan
- Track risk status weekly
- Escalate high-impact risks

=========================================================

3. ISSUE MANAGEMENT

What is it?
Issue is a problem that is happening RIGHT NOW.

Examples:
- Production bug
- API failure
- Environment downtime

TPM ROLE:
- Assign owner
- Track resolution
- Ensure closure
- Communicate impact

=========================================================

4. CHANGE MANAGEMENT (SCOPE CHANGE)

What is it?
Change management handles modifications in project scope
after planning has started.

Examples:
- New feature request
- Priority change
- Requirement update

TPM ROLE:
- Analyze impact on timeline
- Evaluate resource impact
- Get approval from Product Owner / Business
- Update Jira and plan

RULE:
No change without impact analysis.

=========================================================

5. STAKEHOLDER MANAGEMENT STRATEGY

Key stakeholders:
- Product Owner
- Business Unit Head
- Engineering Team
- QA Team
- Business Leaders

TPM APPROACH:

A. Identify stakeholders
B. Understand their expectations
C. Define communication frequency
D. Provide right level of detail

Communication levels:
- Executives → High-level status only
- Product Owner → Feature + sprint level
- Teams → Task-level details

=========================================================

6. RAID LOG (VERY IMPORTANT TPM TOOL)

RAID = Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies

Used to track all critical project factors in one place.

Structure:

R - Risks
A - Assumptions
I - Issues
D - Dependencies

TPM ROLE:
- Maintain RAID log in Excel or Confluence
- Update weekly
- Review in status meetings

=========================================================

7. TPM INTERVIEW ANSWER

Q: What is RAID in TPM?

Answer:

RAID stands for Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies.
It is a key project management tool used to track all critical
factors affecting project delivery. As a TPM, I maintain and
update RAID regularly to ensure risks are mitigated, issues
are resolved, dependencies are tracked, and assumptions are
validated.

=========================================================

8. ONE-LINE SUMMARY

Advanced TPM work focuses on managing dependencies, risks,
issues, changes, and stakeholders using structured tracking
tools like RAID.
