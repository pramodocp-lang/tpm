=========================================================
SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) - Interview Notes
=========================================================

1. What is SAFe?
-----------------
SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) is a framework that helps large organizations
implement Agile practices across multiple teams, programs, and portfolios.

Interview Definition:
"SAFe is a framework that enables organizations to scale Agile by coordinating
multiple Agile teams, aligning business objectives with engineering execution,
and delivering value faster."

---------------------------------------------------------
2. Why SAFe?
---------------------------------------------------------

Scrum works well for a single Agile team.

When an organization has:
- Multiple Scrum teams
- Hundreds of engineers
- Shared dependencies
- Large products

SAFe provides:
- Better coordination
- Common planning
- Shared objectives
- Predictable delivery

---------------------------------------------------------
3. Four Levels of SAFe
---------------------------------------------------------

A. Team Level
-------------
Focus:
- Individual Agile teams

Activities:
- Sprint Planning
- Daily Stand-up
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

Output:
- Working software every sprint

---------------------------------------------------------

B. Program Level (Agile Release Train - ART)
--------------------------------------------

Focus:
- Multiple Agile teams working together

Key Concepts:
- Agile Release Train (ART)
- Program Increment (PI)
- PI Planning
- System Demo

Output:
- Coordinated delivery across teams

---------------------------------------------------------

C. Large Solution Level
-----------------------

Used when:
- One Agile Release Train is not enough
- Very large and complex systems

Examples:
- Banking platforms
- Telecom systems
- Aerospace software

---------------------------------------------------------

D. Portfolio Level
------------------

Focus:
- Business strategy
- Investment decisions
- Budget allocation
- Prioritization

Output:
- Alignment between business goals and execution

---------------------------------------------------------
4. Agile Release Train (ART)
---------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
A long-lived team of Agile teams working together toward a common business goal.

Typical Size:
- 5 to 12 Agile teams
- 50 to 125 people

Purpose:
- Synchronize planning
- Manage dependencies
- Deliver together

---------------------------------------------------------
5. Program Increment (PI)
---------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
A planning and delivery cycle in SAFe.

Duration:
- 8 to 12 weeks
- Usually 4 to 6 sprints

Activities:
- Planning
- Development
- Testing
- Demo
- Delivery

---------------------------------------------------------
6. PI Planning
---------------------------------------------------------

Purpose:
All Agile teams come together to plan the upcoming Program Increment.

Activities:
- Understand business objectives
- Break Features into User Stories
- Identify dependencies
- Estimate work
- Commit to objectives
- Discuss risks

Outputs:
- PI Objectives
- Team commitments
- Dependency map
- Risk list

---------------------------------------------------------
7. Important Roles
---------------------------------------------------------

Product Manager
- Owns program priorities
- Defines Features

Product Owner
- Owns Team Backlog
- Prioritizes User Stories

Scrum Master
- Facilitates Agile ceremonies
- Removes blockers

Release Train Engineer (RTE)
- Chief Scrum Master for the Agile Release Train
- Coordinates all teams
- Removes cross-team impediments

System Architect
- Defines technical architecture
- Guides technical decisions

Business Owners
- Define business goals
- Approve priorities

---------------------------------------------------------
8. Key SAFe Artifacts
---------------------------------------------------------

- Portfolio Backlog
- Program Backlog
- Team Backlog
- Epics
- Features
- User Stories
- PI Objectives
- Roadmap

---------------------------------------------------------
9. Benefits of SAFe
---------------------------------------------------------

- Better cross-team coordination
- Improved visibility
- Faster delivery
- Effective dependency management
- Business and engineering alignment
- Predictable releases

---------------------------------------------------------
10. Challenges of SAFe
---------------------------------------------------------

- Initial learning curve
- More planning overhead
- Requires organizational commitment
- Can become process-heavy if not implemented well

---------------------------------------------------------
11. SAFe vs Scrum
---------------------------------------------------------

Scrum
------
- Single Agile team
- Sprint Planning
- Scrum Master
- Product Owner
- Suitable for small projects

SAFe
-----
- Multiple Agile teams
- PI Planning + Sprint Planning
- Release Train Engineer (RTE)
- Product Manager + Product Owner
- Suitable for enterprise-scale projects

---------------------------------------------------------
12. TPM Responsibilities in SAFe
---------------------------------------------------------

- Lead PI Planning activities
- Manage cross-team dependencies
- Track risks and RAID items
- Coordinate multiple Agile teams
- Facilitate stakeholder communication
- Monitor program delivery
- Ensure alignment with business objectives
- Support release planning and execution

---------------------------------------------------------
13. Interview Answer (60 Seconds)
---------------------------------------------------------

"SAFe, or Scaled Agile Framework, is an enterprise Agile framework that helps
organizations scale Agile practices across multiple teams. Unlike Scrum, which
focuses on a single team, SAFe coordinates many Agile teams through Agile
Release Trains (ARTs) and Program Increments (PIs). Teams participate in PI
Planning to align on business goals, identify dependencies, estimate work,
and commit to shared objectives. As a Technical Project Manager, I would
facilitate PI Planning, manage cross-team dependencies, track risks, align
stakeholders, and ensure successful program execution and predictable
delivery."
