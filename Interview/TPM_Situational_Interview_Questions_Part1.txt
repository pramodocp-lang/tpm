=========================================================
TPM SITUATIONAL INTERVIEW QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (PART 1)
Technical Program Manager Interview Preparation
=========================================================

1. A project is behind schedule. What would you do?

Answer:
- Analyze the root cause of the delay.
- Review the project plan and identify the critical path.
- Meet with the team to understand blockers.
- Reprioritize tasks based on business priority.
- Allocate additional resources if available.
- Update stakeholders with a revised timeline.
- Track progress daily until the project is back on schedule.

---------------------------------------------------------

2. A developer says they cannot complete a task on time.

Answer:
- Understand the reason for the delay.
- Identify whether it is a technical issue, dependency, or resource constraint.
- Help remove blockers.
- Reassign work if necessary.
- Inform stakeholders if delivery is impacted.
- Monitor the task until completion.

---------------------------------------------------------

3. Two team members disagree on a technical solution.

Answer:
- Listen to both perspectives.
- Review business requirements and technical constraints.
- Compare the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.
- Consult the Technical Lead or Solution Architect if needed.
- Make a decision based on scalability, maintainability, cost, and business value.

---------------------------------------------------------

4. A stakeholder keeps changing requirements.

Answer:
- Understand the business reason for the change.
- Assess the impact on scope, timeline, budget, and resources.
- Discuss trade-offs with stakeholders.
- Follow the formal change management process.
- Update the backlog, documentation, and project plan after approval.

---------------------------------------------------------

5. Production goes down during a release.

Answer:
- Initiate the incident response process.
- Notify stakeholders immediately.
- Coordinate with Development, QA, and DevOps teams.
- Roll back the deployment if required.
- Restore service as quickly as possible.
- Conduct a Root Cause Analysis (RCA).
- Implement preventive actions to avoid recurrence.

---------------------------------------------------------

6. A customer reports a high-priority production issue.

Answer:
- Assess the severity and business impact.
- Assign the issue to the engineering team immediately.
- Keep the customer informed with regular updates.
- Track progress until resolution.
- Validate the fix before deployment.
- Conduct RCA and document lessons learned.

---------------------------------------------------------

7. Your team consistently misses Sprint commitments.

Answer:
- Review sprint metrics and velocity.
- Analyze estimation accuracy.
- Identify recurring blockers.
- Improve backlog refinement.
- Coach the team on realistic planning.
- Monitor improvements over future sprints.

---------------------------------------------------------

8. Senior management requests an unrealistic deadline.

Answer:
- Review project estimates and dependencies.
- Present data supporting a realistic timeline.
- Explain project risks.
- Suggest phased delivery or an MVP approach.
- Align expectations with stakeholders.

---------------------------------------------------------

9. A critical team member resigns during the project.

Answer:
- Assess the impact on project delivery.
- Reassign responsibilities.
- Conduct knowledge transfer sessions.
- Update the project schedule.
- Inform stakeholders if timelines are affected.
- Cross-train remaining team members.

---------------------------------------------------------

10. The client is unhappy with project progress.

Answer:
- Listen carefully to their concerns.
- Share transparent project status.
- Explain corrective actions.
- Increase communication frequency.
- Deliver quick wins where possible.
- Rebuild stakeholder confidence through regular updates.

=========================================================
Interview Tip

Whenever possible, answer using the STAR Method.

Situation:
Briefly explain the project context.

Task:
Explain your responsibility.

Action:
Describe the steps you took.

Result:
Share the measurable outcome.

Example:
"We had a critical release with multiple dependencies.
As the TPM, I coordinated engineering, QA, and DevOps teams,
tracked risks using a RAID Log, removed blockers through daily
stand-ups, and communicated progress to stakeholders.
As a result, we delivered the release on schedule with no
critical production issues."
