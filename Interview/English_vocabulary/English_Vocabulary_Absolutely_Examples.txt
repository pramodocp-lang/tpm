# English Vocabulary: ABSOLUTELY

Meaning:
"Absolutely" is an adverb that means:

* Completely
* Totally
* Definitely
* Without any doubt

---

1. To Show Strong Agreement

---

Examples:

* Absolutely! I agree with you.
* Absolutely! That's a great idea.
* A: Can you help me tomorrow?
  B: Absolutely!
* A: Should we start the meeting now?
  B: Absolutely.

---

2. To Mean "Completely" or "Totally"

---

Examples:

* The room was absolutely silent.
* She was absolutely right.
* The movie was absolutely amazing.
* I am absolutely certain about my decision.
* The task is absolutely complete.

---

3. To Emphasize an Adjective

---

Examples:

* This cake is absolutely delicious.
* The weather is absolutely beautiful today.
* He was absolutely exhausted after work.
* The presentation was absolutely fantastic.
* The solution is absolutely perfect.

---

4. To Express Certainty

---

Examples:

* I will absolutely attend the meeting.
* We absolutely need more resources.
* You are absolutely correct.
* This approach will absolutely improve efficiency.
* I absolutely believe in continuous learning.

---

5. Professional Workplace Examples

---

* This feature is absolutely critical for the release.
* Customer satisfaction is absolutely our priority.
* We absolutely appreciate your feedback.
* The deadline is absolutely non-negotiable.
* This risk must absolutely be addressed.

---

6. Common Collocations

---

* Absolutely right
* Absolutely correct
* Absolutely sure

* Absolutely essential
* Absolutely impossible
* Absolutely amazing
* Absolutely fantastic
* Absolutely beautiful
* Absolutely perfect
* Absolutely delighted

---

7. Daily Conversation Examples

---

Friend:
A: Are you coming to the party?
B: Absolutely!

Office:
A: Can you finish this report today?
B: Absolutely.

Restaurant:
A: Was the food good?
B: It was absolutely delicious.

Travel:
A: Would you visit Japan again?
B: Absolutely! I loved it.

Shopping:
A: Is this worth buying?
B: Absolutely.

---

8. TPM (Technical Program Manager) Examples

---

* We absolutely need stakeholder approval before deployment.
* This dependency absolutely impacts the release schedule.
* The engineering team is absolutely committed to the milestone.
* Risk mitigation is absolutely essential for project success.
* We absolutely must complete testing before production rollout.

---

9. Interview Examples

---

Q: Are you comfortable managing cross-functional teams?
A: Absolutely. I have experience coordinating engineering, product, QA, and business stakeholders.

Q: Can you handle multiple priorities?
A: Absolutely. I prioritize work based on business impact and project timelines.

---

10. Simple Conversation

---

A: Is this your final decision?
B: Absolutely.

A: Can I trust you?
B: Absolutely.

A: Was the presentation successful?
B: Absolutely! Everyone appreciated it.

---

## Quick Tip

"Absolutely" is stronger than "very" or "really."

Examples:

* I'm very happy.
* I'm really happy.
* I'm absolutely delighted. (Strongest emphasis)

Use "absolutely" when you want to express complete confidence, certainty, or strong agreement.
