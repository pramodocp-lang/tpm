Q3. A vendor has delayed API integration and the project is behind schedule. What would you do?

Answer:

First, I would assess the impact of the vendor delay on the project timeline and identify which deliverables are affected.

Then, I would immediately connect with the vendor to understand the root cause, obtain a realistic ETA, and escalate the issue if required.

In parallel, I would work with the Engineering, QA, and UAT teams to reprioritize the sprint and continue with other tasks that are not blocked.

I would proactively communicate the situation, mitigation plan, and revised timeline to stakeholders so there are no surprises.

Finally, I would closely monitor the vendor's progress until the issue is resolved, 
ensuring the impact on the project timeline and business objectives is kept to a minimum.

==========================================================

Q4. How do you ensure a project is delivered on time, within scope, and within budget?

Answer:

To ensure a project is delivered on time, within scope, and within budget, I focus on proper planning, execution, and continuous monitoring.

First, I work with stakeholders to clearly define the project scope, requirements, 
timelines, budget, and success criteria so everyone has the same expectations.

Next, I create a realistic project plan, estimate resources, and break the work into manageable sprints with clear priorities and ownership.

During execution, I track progress through daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, 
Jira dashboards, burndown charts, and project KPIs. I identify risks and dependencies early and take corrective actions before they impact the schedule.

I also maintain regular communication with Product, Engineering, QA, DevOps, 
UAT teams, vendors, and business stakeholders to provide status updates, resolve blockers quickly, and manage any change requests through proper impact analysis.

Finally, I closely monitor the project timeline, scope, budget, and quality throughout the lifecycle to ensure successful delivery and customer satisfaction.

==========================================================

Q5. Tell me about a project that went off track. What happened, and what did you do?

Answer:

In one of my projects, we started falling behind schedule because the client introduced new requirements during development, 
and an external API vendor also delayed their integration.

As the Technical Project Manager, my responsibility was to minimize the impact and keep the project on track.

I first assessed the impact on the timeline and identified the critical tasks that were blocked.

Then, I coordinated with the Product Owner to prioritize the new requirements and worked with the 
Engineering team to continue development on features that were not dependent on the vendor.

I stayed in regular contact with the vendor, escalated the issue when required, 
and kept stakeholders informed with transparent status updates and a revised delivery plan.

As a result, we reduced the delay, completed the remaining work efficiently, 
and delivered the project with only a minor schedule impact while maintaining the expected quality.
