=========================================================
Interview Improvement Plan
Senior TPM Communication
=========================================================

Observation:
During the last part of the interview, I answered some questions in very short sentences or provided only numbers and facts.

Example:
Q: How many team members do you manage?
Answer: 15.

This answer is correct, but it does not demonstrate leadership or provide context.

=========================================================
What Interviewers Expect
=========================================================

A Technical Project Manager should answer with:

1. Fact
2. Context
3. Responsibility
4. Business Impact (when applicable)

=========================================================
Communication Formula
=========================================================

Fact
+
Context
+
Responsibility
+
Outcome (Optional)

=========================================================
Example 1
=========================================================

Question:
How many people are on your team?

Weak Answer:
15.

Strong Answer:
My core delivery team consists of around 15 members, 
including Backend Developers, 
Frontend Developers, 
QA Engineers, 
DevOps Engineers, 
and Business Analysts. 
My responsibility is to coordinate cross-functional teams,
remove blockers,
and ensure timely project delivery.

---------------------------------------------------------

Example 2

Question:
How many projects have you managed?

Weak Answer:
6.

Strong Answer:
I have managed around six major software projects, primarily SaaS and enterprise applications.
I was responsible for project planning, stakeholder management, sprint execution, risk management, and successful delivery.

---------------------------------------------------------

Example 3

Question:
How many years of experience do you have?

Weak Answer:
14 Years.

Strong Answer:
I have over 14 years of experience in software engineering,
including more than 6 years as a Technical Project Manager,
leading cross-functional teams,
driving Agile delivery,
and managing end-to-end project execution.

=========================================================
Leadership Language
=========================================================

Instead of saying:

"We completed the project."

Say:

"I led the delivery of the project."

---------------------------------------------------------

Instead of saying:

"We discussed the issue."

Say:

"I facilitated the discussion between Engineering and Product teams to reach a practical solution."

---------------------------------------------------------

Instead of saying:

"We fixed the problem."

Say:

"I coordinated with the Engineering team, identified the root cause, prioritized the issue, and ensured timely resolution."

=========================================================
Power Words for Senior TPM
=========================================================

- Led
- Drove
- Facilitated
- Coordinated
- Planned
- Managed
- Aligned
- Prioritized
- Evaluated
- Collaborated
- Delivered
- Mitigated
- Resolved
- Ensured
- Influenced
- Mentored
- Communicated
- Executed

=========================================================
Interview Rule
=========================================================

Do not stop after giving the fact.

Always add:

✓ Context
✓ Responsibility
✓ Business Value

=========================================================
Goal
=========================================================

Every answer should make the interviewer feel:

"This person sounds like a Senior Technical Project Manager who takes ownership, leads teams, and delivers business outcomes."

=========================================================
My Focus for the Next Interview
=========================================================

✓ Speak calmly and confidently.
✓ Use simple, clear English.
✓ Expand short answers naturally.
✓ Demonstrate leadership and ownership.
✓ Explain the "why" behind my decisions.
✓ End answers with the business outcome whenever possible.
