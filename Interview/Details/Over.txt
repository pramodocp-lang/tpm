OVER

Definition:
"Over" is a preposition, adverb, adjective, or prefix. It is commonly used to show position, movement, completion, transfer, control, or an amount greater than something.

--------------------------------------------------
1. POSITION (Above)
--------------------------------------------------
The fan is over the table.
The plane flew over the city.
There is a bridge over the river.

--------------------------------------------------
2. MOVEMENT (Across)
--------------------------------------------------
Jump over the fence.
Walk over the bridge.
Come over to my house.

--------------------------------------------------
3. COMPLETION (Finished)
--------------------------------------------------
The meeting is over.
The interview is over.
The project is over.
The game is over.

--------------------------------------------------
4. MORE THAN (Greater than)
--------------------------------------------------
There are over 100 employees.
She is over 30 years old.
The meeting lasted over two hours.

--------------------------------------------------
5. TRANSFER (Handing something)
--------------------------------------------------
Hand over the documents.
Turn over the keys.
Please hand over your ID card.

--------------------------------------------------
6. REVIEW (Check carefully)
--------------------------------------------------
Go over the report.
Look over the document.
Read over the email.
Think it over.

--------------------------------------------------
7. CONTROL / RESPONSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------
She has control over the team.
He has authority over the project.
I have no control over the situation.

--------------------------------------------------
8. COMMUNICATION
--------------------------------------------------
We spoke over the phone.
Let's discuss it over lunch.
We talked over a video call.
