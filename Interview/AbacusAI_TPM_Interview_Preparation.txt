Q2. Walk me through your recent project.

Explain:

• Project Overview
• Business Goal
• Team Size
• Your Responsibilities
• Technologies Used
• Challenges
• Results
• Lessons Learned

Project Overview
My recent project was a SaaS-based business management platform that helped organizations manage their day-to-day operations through a centralized web application. It included modules for user management, workflow automation, reporting, and role-based access.
Business Goal
The goal was to replace manual processes with a scalable digital platform, improve operational efficiency, and provide real-time visibility into business data.
Team Size
The project team consisted of around 10 members:
5 Backend Developers
2 Frontend Developers
2 QA Engineers
1 UI/UX Designer
I worked as the Technical Lead, coordinating with product owners and stakeholders.
My Responsibilities
Planned project execution and sprint deliverables.
Broke requirements into technical tasks.
Coordinated developers, QA, and product teams.
Managed risks, dependencies, and release timelines.
Reviewed code and ensured coding standards.
Mentored team members and resolved technical blockers.
Provided regular project status updates to stakeholders.
Technologies Used
PHP, Laravel
MySQL
REST APIs
JavaScript
Git
AWS (EC2, S3)
Docker
Jira
Challenges The biggest challenge was changing business requirements during development while maintaining delivery timelines. We prioritized features, refined the backlog, improved stakeholder communication, and released high-priority functionality first to stay on schedule.
Results
Delivered the project on time.
Improved system performance and scalability.
Reduced manual work through automation.
Received positive feedback from business stakeholders.
Established a stable foundation for future feature enhancements.
Lessons Learned The project reinforced that successful delivery depends not only on technical execution but also on clear communication, proactive risk management, and continuous collaboration across cross-functional teams. Early stakeholder alignment and regular feedback significantly reduce rework and delivery risks.
