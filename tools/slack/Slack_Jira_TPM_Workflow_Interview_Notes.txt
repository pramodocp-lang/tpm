JIRA + SLACK WORKFLOW IN REAL PROJECTS

1. Requirement / Ticket Creation (Jira)
- Product/Business creates story or task in Jira
- TPM ensures requirement clarity and prioritization

2. Slack Notification Trigger
- Jira sends automatic notification to Slack channel (e.g. #project-alpha)
- Team gets instant visibility of new ticket

3. Discussion in Slack
- Engineers, QA, TPM discuss requirement in Slack thread
- Clarifications and decisions happen in real time

4. Jira Update from Slack Discussion
- Based on discussion, Jira ticket is updated:
  - status changes (To Do → In Progress)
  - comments added
  - assignee updated

5. Sprint Execution Tracking
- Daily updates shared in Slack (standup channels)
- Jira dashboard reflects sprint progress

6. Issue Escalation
- If blocker occurs:
  - Slack alert sent to #incident or #project channel
  - TPM coordinates resolution with stakeholders

7. CI/CD & Dev Updates
- GitHub/Jenkins integrated with Slack
- Deployment, build failures, or success notifications posted

8. Sprint Review & Closure
- Jira tickets moved to Done
- Slack channel used for final updates and summary
- Channel may be archived after release

FLOW SUMMARY
Jira Ticket → Slack Notification → Discussion → Jira Update → Execution → Alerts → Deployment → Closure
