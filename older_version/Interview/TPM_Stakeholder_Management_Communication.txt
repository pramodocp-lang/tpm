Q. How do you approach stakeholder management and communication in complex projects?

A.
I approach stakeholder management and communication in complex projects through structured identification, clear alignment, and continuous engagement.

First, I identify all stakeholders and categorize them based on their influence and interest using a stakeholder matrix (high power vs high interest). 
This helps me understand who needs frequent updates and who needs periodic communication.

Next, I establish clear expectations early in the project by aligning on:
- Project scope and objectives
- Roles and responsibilities
- Communication cadence
- Decision-making process

For communication, I tailor the approach based on stakeholder type:
- Executive stakeholders: High-level dashboards, milestones, risks, and business impact
- Product/Business teams: Feature progress, priorities, and delivery timelines
- Engineering/QA teams: Daily standups, sprint planning, blockers, and technical details

I maintain transparency by proactively sharing:
- Progress updates
- Risks and mitigation plans
- Dependencies and blockers

For complex projects, I also use structured communication channels such as:
- Weekly status reports
- RAID logs (Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies)
- Sprint ceremonies (standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Ad-hoc escalation calls when needed

Example:
If a critical dependency is delayed from another team, I immediately communicate impact to stakeholders, present mitigation options, and realign timelines collaboratively instead of waiting for escalation.

Finally, I ensure continuous feedback loops so stakeholders feel engaged and informed throughout the project lifecycle, which helps build trust and avoid surprises.
