=========================================================
WHY, WHAT, AND HOW A TPM COMMUNICATES WITH BUSINESS LEADERS
=========================================================

1. WHO ARE BUSINESS LEADERS?

Business leaders are decision-makers responsible for
business outcomes and strategic direction.

Examples:
- CEO
- CTO
- CIO
- VP Engineering
- VP Product
- Director
- Business Unit Head

=========================================================

2. WHY DOES A TPM COMMUNICATE WITH BUSINESS LEADERS?

A TPM communicates with business leaders to:

1. Provide Project Status
2. Report Risks and Issues
3. Escalate Blockers
4. Share Progress Against Goals
5. Align Technical Work with Business Objectives
6. Support Decision Making
7. Ensure Stakeholder Visibility

Without regular communication, leadership may not know:
- Project health
- Delivery risks
- Budget concerns
- Timeline impacts

=========================================================

3. WHAT DOES A TPM COMMUNICATE?

A. Project Status

Examples:
- Green
- Yellow
- Red

Example:
Project Status = Green

Meaning:
Project is progressing as planned.

---------------------------------------------------------

B. Milestone Progress

Examples:
- Requirements Completed
- Design Completed
- Development 80% Complete
- Testing Started

---------------------------------------------------------

C. Risks

Examples:
- Resource shortage
- Vendor dependency
- Technical complexity

Example:
Risk:
Key developer unavailable next month.

---------------------------------------------------------

D. Issues

Examples:
- Environment outage
- Production defect
- Delayed API delivery

---------------------------------------------------------

E. Schedule Updates

Examples:
- On Track
- Delayed by 1 Week
- Ahead of Schedule

---------------------------------------------------------

F. Budget Updates

Examples:
- Budget On Track
- Budget Overrun Risk

---------------------------------------------------------

G. Key Metrics

Examples:
- Sprint Velocity
- Defect Count
- Completion Percentage
- Resource Utilization

=========================================================

4. HOW DOES A TPM COMMUNICATE?

A. Weekly Status Reports

Sent to:
- Leadership
- Product Managers
- Engineering Managers

Contains:
- Progress
- Risks
- Issues
- Next Steps

---------------------------------------------------------

B. Steering Committee Meetings

Purpose:
Review major project updates and decisions.

Topics:
- Timeline
- Budget
- Risks
- Dependencies

---------------------------------------------------------

C. Executive Dashboards

Tools:
- Excel
- Power BI
- Jira Dashboards

Shows:
- Project Health
- Milestones
- Delivery Metrics

---------------------------------------------------------

D. Email Updates

Used for:
- Escalations
- Decisions
- Important Announcements

---------------------------------------------------------

E. Leadership Reviews

Monthly or Quarterly Reviews

Purpose:
- Strategic alignment
- Business impact review
- Portfolio updates

=========================================================

5. COMMUNICATION STYLE FOR BUSINESS LEADERS

Business leaders care about:

- Outcomes
- Timelines
- Risks
- Costs
- Business Impact

They usually do NOT need:
- Detailed code discussions
- Technical implementation details

Example:

Bad Update:
"The API authentication layer has OAuth token
refresh issues."

Good Update:
"Login functionality may be delayed by 3 days due
to a technical issue. The team is implementing a fix."

=========================================================

6. TPM RESPONSIBILITY

The TPM acts as a bridge between:

Business Leaders
        ↕
Product Team
        ↕
Engineering Team
        ↕
QA Team
        ↕
Operations Team

The TPM translates technical progress into business
language that leaders can understand.

=========================================================

7. TPM INTERVIEW ANSWER

Q: Why do you communicate with business leaders?

Answer:

I communicate with business leaders to provide visibility
into project progress, risks, issues, timelines, and
business impact. My goal is to ensure alignment between
technical execution and business objectives while enabling
leadership to make informed decisions.

=========================================================

8. ONE-LINE INTERVIEW ANSWER

A TPM communicates with business leaders to provide
project visibility, manage expectations, highlight risks,
and ensure technical delivery aligns with business goals.
