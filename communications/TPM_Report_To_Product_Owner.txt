=========================================================
TPM REPORTING TO PRODUCT OWNER
=========================================================

1. WHO IS A PRODUCT OWNER?

The Product Owner (PO) is responsible for maximizing
product value and managing the product backlog.

Responsibilities:
- Define product requirements
- Prioritize backlog
- Clarify user stories
- Accept completed work
- Represent business needs

=========================================================

2. WHY DOES A TPM REPORT TO THE PRODUCT OWNER?

A TPM reports to the Product Owner to:

1. Provide delivery updates
2. Discuss sprint progress
3. Highlight risks and blockers
4. Review dependencies
5. Align development with product goals
6. Support release planning
7. Manage stakeholder expectations

=========================================================

3. WHAT DOES A TPM REPORT?

A. Sprint Progress

Examples:
- Sprint completion status
- Stories completed
- Stories in progress
- Blocked items

---------------------------------------------------------

B. Delivery Status

Examples:
- On Track
- At Risk
- Delayed

---------------------------------------------------------

C. Risks

Examples:
- Resource constraints
- Technical challenges
- Vendor dependencies

---------------------------------------------------------

D. Issues

Examples:
- Environment problems
- Defects
- Integration failures

---------------------------------------------------------

E. Dependencies

Examples:
- Waiting for API completion
- Waiting for design approval
- Waiting for external vendor

---------------------------------------------------------

F. Release Readiness

Examples:
- Development complete
- Testing complete
- Open defects
- Go-live risks

=========================================================

4. HOW DOES A TPM COMMUNICATE WITH THE PRODUCT OWNER?

A. Daily Sync Meetings

Discuss:
- Progress
- Blockers
- Priorities

---------------------------------------------------------

B. Sprint Planning

Discuss:
- Sprint scope
- Capacity
- Dependencies

---------------------------------------------------------

C. Backlog Refinement

Discuss:
- Story readiness
- Acceptance criteria
- Priorities

---------------------------------------------------------

D. Sprint Review

Discuss:
- Completed work
- Demonstrations
- Business value delivered

---------------------------------------------------------

E. Status Reports

Include:
- Progress update
- Risks
- Issues
- Next steps

=========================================================

5. COMMUNICATION STYLE WITH PRODUCT OWNER

Product Owners care about:

- Feature delivery
- Sprint commitments
- Business value
- Release dates
- Customer impact

Focus on:
- Outcomes
- Progress
- Risks
- Decisions needed

=========================================================

6. SAMPLE UPDATE TO PRODUCT OWNER

Sprint Status: Green

Completed:
- Student Registration Module
- Lead Management API

In Progress:
- Mentor Assignment Feature

Risks:
- Third-party API dependency

Issues:
- None

Expected Sprint Completion:
- On Track

=========================================================

7. TPM INTERVIEW ANSWER

Q: How do you communicate with a Product Owner?

Answer:

I work closely with the Product Owner to provide updates
on sprint progress, delivery timelines, risks, issues,
and dependencies. I ensure that engineering execution
remains aligned with product priorities and business
objectives. Through regular meetings and status reports,
I help the Product Owner make informed decisions and
manage stakeholder expectations.

=========================================================

8. ONE-LINE INTERVIEW ANSWER

A TPM communicates with the Product Owner to ensure
delivery progress, risks, dependencies, and technical
execution remain aligned with product priorities and
business goals.
