TPM and Design Team
or
How does a TPM work with the Design Team?

3) Answer:

A Technical Program Manager (TPM) works closely with the 
Design Team (UX/UI designers) to ensure that product design is feasible, aligned with requirements, 
and delivered on time with engineering.


Why TPM communicates with the Design Team:

1. Requirement alignment
TPM ensures designers clearly understand:

Product vision
User requirements
Business goals

2. Design feasibility check
TPM helps validate:

Whether the design can be built within technical constraints
Any engineering limitations early in the process

3. Timeline coordination
TPM aligns:

Design delivery dates (wireframes, mockups, prototypes)
Sprint planning and development schedule

4. Dependency management
TPM tracks dependencies like:

Design completion before development starts
Design changes impacting engineering work

5. Change control
If design changes happen, TPM:

Assesses impact on scope, timeline, and effort
Communicates updates to engineering and stakeholders

6. Cross-team communication
TPM ensures smooth collaboration between:

Product Manager (what to build)
Design Team (how it should look/feel)
Engineering (how to build it)
Simple interview line:

A TPM works with the Design Team to ensure user experience designs are feasible, 
aligned with requirements, and delivered on time without impacting engineering timelines.
