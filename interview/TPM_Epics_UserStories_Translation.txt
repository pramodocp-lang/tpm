Q. How have you translated product concepts into user stories and epics in your projects?

A.
I translate product concepts into epics and user stories using a structured Agile breakdown approach.

First, I understand the product concept by working with Product Managers and stakeholders to capture the business goal, user problem, and expected outcome.

Then I convert the high-level concept into EPICS, which represent major features or end-to-end user journeys aligned to business value.

After that, I break each epic into USER STORIES using the format:
As a <user>, I want <need>, so that <value>.

Each user story includes clear ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA to make it testable and development-ready.

I collaborate with Engineering and QA to ensure:
- Technical feasibility
- Dependencies are identified
- Edge cases are covered
- Stories are properly estimated and prioritized

Example:
Product Concept: Improve onboarding experience

Epics:
- User registration flow
- Profile setup
- Guided onboarding experience

User Stories:
- As a user, I want to sign up using email so that I can access the system
- As a new user, I want a guided onboarding flow so that I can understand the product quickly

Finally, I ensure full traceability from 
product concept → epics → user stories → sprint backlog to maintain alignment with business goals and delivery outcomes.
