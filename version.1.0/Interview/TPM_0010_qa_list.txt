“Why does a TPM communicate with QA, and what do they discuss?”

3) Answer:
A Technical Program Manager (TPM) communicates closely with QA (Quality Assurance) because QA is responsible for validating product quality before release, and TPM ensures the entire delivery process stays on track.

Why TPM communicates with QA:

A TPM acts as a bridge between engineering, product, and QA teams to make sure releases are smooth, predictable, and high quality.

Key reasons:

1. Requirement clarity
TPM ensures QA clearly understands:

Business requirements
Acceptance criteria
Edge cases and expected behavior

2. Test planning alignment
TPM coordinates:

Test scope (what to test / what not to test)
Test timelines aligned with sprint/release
Resource availability

3. Risk tracking
TPM works with QA to identify:

High-risk modules
Regression risks
Release blockers

4. Defect management
TPM helps track:

Critical bugs
Priority of fixes
Release go/no-go decisions

5. Release readiness
TPM uses QA input to decide:

Is testing complete?
Are critical bugs resolved?
Can we safely release?
In simple terms:

TPM talks to QA to ensure “we are building the right product, and it is tested properly before going live.”
