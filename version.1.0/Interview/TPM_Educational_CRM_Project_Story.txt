============================================================
EDUCATIONAL CRM TPM PROJECT STORY (BANKING/E-COM/SaaS STYLE)
============================================================

SITUATION:
I led the development and delivery of an Educational CRM System designed for managing student admissions, counselling, branch operations, mentors, and centralized head-office reporting.

The system had multiple roles like Counsellor, Front Office, Branch Office, Mentor, and Admin, and required integration across workflows, dashboards, and reporting modules.

============================================================

TASK:
My responsibility was to manage end-to-end delivery of the CRM platform, ensure SDLC compliance, coordinate multiple cross-functional teams, and deliver features across iterative Agile releases.

I also needed to manage risks, dependencies, resource allocation, and ensure alignment between business stakeholders and engineering teams.

============================================================

ACTION:

1. Project Planning, Execution & Delivery:
- Defined end-to-end roadmap for CRM modules like admissions, lead tracking, counseling, and reporting.
- Broke down delivery into phased milestones and tracked execution progress.

2. SDLC Management & Delivery Governance:
- Ensured structured SDLC flow from requirement gathering to production deployment.
- Introduced governance checkpoints for design approval, QA sign-off, and release readiness.

3. Agile / Scrum / Hybrid Execution:
- Executed delivery using Agile sprints with structured sprint planning and backlog grooming.
- Managed hybrid release cycles aligned with academic/business timelines.

4. Risk & Dependency Management:
- Identified risks such as integration delays between CRM modules and reporting system.
- Managed dependencies between frontend, backend, QA, and data teams.

5. Sprint & Release Planning:
- Defined sprint scope for CRM features like student onboarding, lead management, and dashboards.
- Ensured smooth release coordination across modules.

6. Resource & Capacity Planning:
- Allocated developers, QA, and DevOps resources based on sprint priorities and workload.
- Balanced capacity across parallel CRM modules.

7. Stakeholder Management:
- Worked closely with business stakeholders from education domain (admissions, branch heads).
- Managed expectations around feature delivery timelines and scope changes.

8. Cross-Functional Leadership:
- Coordinated engineering, QA, UI/UX, and product teams for CRM delivery.
- Resolved priority conflicts between branch operations and central reporting needs.

9. Team Collaboration:
- Ensured strong collaboration across distributed teams.
- Conducted regular sync-ups and ensured blockers were resolved quickly.

============================================================

RESULT:
- Successfully delivered Educational CRM with multi-role support and modular architecture.
- Improved visibility into student lifecycle and admission pipeline.
- Strengthened cross-team collaboration and delivery governance.
- Achieved predictable Agile releases with reduced dependency delays.
- Improved stakeholder satisfaction across branches and head office teams.

============================================================

KEY TPM TAKEAWAY:
- End-to-end CRM delivery ownership
- Strong SDLC governance across modules
- Agile + hybrid execution model
- Effective risk and dependency management
- Cross-functional leadership across education domain teams
