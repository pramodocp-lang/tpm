TPM PROJECT STORY (STAR FORMAT – END-TO-END DELIVERY SCENARIO)

------------------------------------------------------------

SITUATION:
I was responsible for leading the delivery of a large-scale Customer Engagement Platform used across multiple business units. The system involved multiple integrations, distributed engineering teams, and strict timelines aligned with a quarterly business release.

The project had challenges such as unclear initial requirements, multiple team dependencies, tight deadlines, and evolving scope from stakeholders.

------------------------------------------------------------

TASK:
As the Technical Project Manager, my responsibility was to:
- Plan and execute the end-to-end project delivery
- Ensure SDLC compliance and governance
- Manage sprint and release planning
- Handle cross-team dependencies and risks
- Align stakeholders across business and engineering teams
- Ensure on-time, high-quality delivery within scope

------------------------------------------------------------

ACTION:

1. Project Planning, Execution & Delivery:
- Created a detailed project plan with milestones, dependencies, and delivery timelines.
- Broke down work into phases and tracked execution using weekly status reviews.

2. SDLC Management & Delivery Governance:
- Ensured all SDLC phases (requirements, design, development, testing, deployment) were followed.
- Introduced structured governance checkpoints (design review, QA sign-off, release readiness).

3. Agile / Scrum / Hybrid Execution:
- Worked in a hybrid model combining Agile sprints with fixed release deadlines.
- Facilitated sprint planning, backlog grooming, and retrospectives.

4. Risk & Dependency Management:
- Identified risks such as integration delays and resource constraints early.
- Maintained a RAID log and actively tracked mitigation actions.
- Coordinated dependency resolution across teams.

5. Sprint & Release Planning:
- Defined sprint scope with product and engineering teams.
- Managed scope changes carefully without impacting release commitments.
- Coordinated release readiness across all teams.

6. Resource & Capacity Planning:
- Worked with engineering managers to allocate resources based on skill and availability.
- Balanced workloads to avoid bottlenecks and delivery delays.

7. Stakeholder Management:
- Conducted regular status updates with leadership and business stakeholders.
- Managed expectations during scope changes and timeline risks.
- Provided transparent communication on delivery health.

8. Cross-Functional Leadership:
- Coordinated across engineering, QA, product, and DevOps teams.
- Resolved conflicting priorities through alignment discussions.
- Ensured everyone was working toward the same release goal.

9. Team Collaboration:
- Encouraged open communication across distributed teams.
- Ensured daily syncs and strong collaboration during sprint execution.
- Addressed blockers quickly to maintain momentum.

------------------------------------------------------------

RESULT:
- Successfully delivered the platform within the planned release cycle.
- Improved delivery predictability through structured governance.
- Reduced production risks by early identification of dependencies.
- Improved cross-team collaboration and reduced delivery delays.
- Stakeholder confidence increased due to transparent reporting and consistent communication.

------------------------------------------------------------

KEY IMPACT SUMMARY:
- On-time delivery of complex multi-team project
- Strong governance and SDLC compliance
- Effective risk and dependency management
- Smooth Agile + hybrid execution model
- Improved stakeholder alignment and communication
