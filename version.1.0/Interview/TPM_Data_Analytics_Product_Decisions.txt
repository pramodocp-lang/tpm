Q. How do you use data analytics and research to drive product decisions?

A.
I use data analytics and research to ensure product decisions are evidence-based rather than assumption-driven.

First, I identify the business problem or product goal and define key success metrics (KPIs) such as conversion rate, engagement, retention, or drop-off points.

Then I use a combination of:
- Product analytics (user behavior data, funnels, cohort analysis)
- Customer feedback (support tickets, surveys, NPS, user interviews)
- A/B testing results
- Market and competitor research

I analyze user behavior patterns to identify where users are struggling or dropping off in the journey. For example, funnel analysis helps me pinpoint friction points in onboarding or checkout flows.

Based on insights, I collaborate with Product and Engineering teams to:
- Prioritize features that have highest impact on KPIs
- Remove or simplify low-performing steps
- Validate hypotheses through A/B testing before full rollout

I also ensure decisions are continuously validated post-release by tracking metrics in dashboards (e.g., Power BI or product analytics tools) to confirm expected improvements.

Example:
If data shows a high drop-off during signup, I investigate whether the issue is UI complexity or performance. Based on findings, I may propose simplifying the form or adding social login, and then measure improvement through A/B testing.

This approach ensures product decisions are:
- Data-driven
- User-centric
- Continuously optimized based on real-world feedback
