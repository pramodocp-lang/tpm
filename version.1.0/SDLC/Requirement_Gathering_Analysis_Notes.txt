=====================================================
REQUIREMENT GATHERING & ANALYSIS
=====================================================

1. What is Requirement Gathering?
---------------------------------
Requirement Gathering is the process of collecting business needs,
user expectations, functional requirements, and technical constraints
from stakeholders before starting development.

Goal:
Build the right product that solves the customer's problem.

-----------------------------------------------------

2. What is Requirement Analysis?
--------------------------------
Requirement Analysis is the process of reviewing, validating,
prioritizing, refining, and documenting gathered requirements
to ensure they are complete, clear, feasible, and testable.

Goal:
Convert business needs into actionable development requirements.

-----------------------------------------------------

3. Difference

Requirement Gathering
- Collect information
- Understand business needs
- Meet stakeholders
- Ask questions

Requirement Analysis
- Analyze collected information
- Remove ambiguity
- Prioritize requirements
- Check feasibility
- Prepare documentation

-----------------------------------------------------

- Operations Team

↓

Step 2
Understand Business Goal

Questions:

- Why is this project needed?
- Expected business outcome?

↓

Step 3
Gather Requirements

Methods:
- Meetings
- Workshops
- Interviews
- Brainstorming
- Surveys
- Existing documentation
- Observation

↓

Step 4
Document Requirements

Documents:
- BRD
- PRD
- User Stories
- Use Cases
- Process Flow
- Wireframes

↓

Step 5
Validate Requirements

Review with stakeholders

Confirm:
- Correct?
- Complete?
- No missing requirements?

↓

Step 6
Get Approval

Baseline the requirements before development starts.

13. Interview Answer

Q: How do you gather requirements?

Answer:

I begin by identifying all key stakeholders and understanding the
business objectives. I conduct workshops and discussions to collect
functional and non-functional requirements. I document them as user
stories with acceptance criteria, validate them with stakeholders,
prioritize using MoSCoW, identify dependencies and risks, and obtain
approval before development begins. Throughout the project, I manage
requirement changes through a structured change control process.

-----------------------------------------------------

14. Best Practices

Listen actively.

Ask "Why?" multiple times to understand the root need.

Avoid assumptions.

Document everything.

Validate requirements early.

Prioritize based on business value.

Keep stakeholders engaged.

Maintain requirement traceability.

Review requirements before every sprint.

Manage scope changes through formal approval.
